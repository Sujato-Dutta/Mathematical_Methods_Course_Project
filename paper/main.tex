\begin{abstract}
Multiplicative gamma noise severely degrades images in Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) and medical ultrasound systems. Classical partial differential equation (PDE) methods offer mathematical interpretability but limited restoration capacity, while deep convolutional networks like DnCNN achieve high performance at the cost of interpretability and massive parameterization. This paper presents \textbf{Hybrid++}, a novel trainable nonlinear reaction-diffusion architecture that bridges this divide. Hybrid++ combines a fully differentiable PDE initialization with a compact 3-stage reaction-diffusion network featuring 64-channel multiscale filter banks, 4-layer Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) attention-based influence functions, and a 64-dimensional noise level embedding. A two-phase training strategy---stage-wise optimization followed by joint end-to-end refinement---enables tight co-adaptation of all learnable parameters including the PDE physics. On the FoE benchmark, Hybrid++ achieves \textbf{20.86~dB} ($L{=}1$) and \textbf{25.02~dB} ($L{=}10$), outperforming the classical PDE baseline by +3.20~dB and the original TNRD by +2.31~dB, while closing the gap to DnCNN (556k parameters) to within just 0.23~dB using only \textbf{44,408 learnable parameters}---less than 8\% of DnCNN's capacity.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Multiplicative gamma noise poses a fundamental challenge in coherent imaging systems such as Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR), sonar, and medical ultrasound, where the noise is signal-dependent and cannot be addressed by standard additive-noise denoisers. Classical approaches based on nonlinear smooth diffusion partial differential equations (PDEs) \cite{perona1990scale} naturally accommodate this signal-dependence by adapting smoothing strength to local image gradients and intensity. However, these methods rely on fixed, handcrafted parameters and struggle to recover complex textures.

At the opposite extreme, unconstrained deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) such as DnCNN \cite{zhang2017beyond} achieve remarkable denoising performance but operate as opaque black boxes, offering no mathematical insight into the restoration process and requiring hundreds of thousands of parameters. Traditional non-local methods like BM3D \cite{dabov2007bm3d}, while effective for additive Gaussian noise, require ad-hoc adaptations (e.g., homomorphic transforms) for multiplicative noise and often produce suboptimal results in the gamma noise regime.

Trainable Nonlinear Reaction Diffusion (TNRD) \cite{chen2017tnrd} offers an elegant middle ground by unfolding the denoising process as a sequence of interpretable diffusion stages with learnable parameters. However, the original TNRD was designed for additive Gaussian noise and its compact parameterization limits its representational capacity for the more challenging multiplicative gamma model.

The main contribution of this paper is the proposal of \textbf{Hybrid++}, a highly advanced TNRD-based architecture specifically designed for multiplicative gamma noise. Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
\item A fully differentiable PDE initialization module whose physical parameters ($\alpha$, $\beta$) are optimized end-to-end via backpropagation, replacing traditional grid search.
\item Parallel multiscale filter banks (32 filters of $3{\times}3$ and 32 filters of $5{\times}5$) producing 64-channel feature representations that capture both fine textures and broader structural context simultaneously.
\item A 4-layer SE-attention influence function with an expanded squeeze-excitation bottleneck (64$\to$16$\to$64), enabling deep nonlinear feature weighting without normalization layers.
\item A 3-layer MLP noise embedding (1$\to$32$\to$64$\to$64) that conditions every stage on the noise severity, enabling a single unified model to handle varying noise levels.
\item A two-phase training strategy combining stage-wise optimization with joint end-to-end refinement for tight co-adaptation of all parameters including the PDE physics.
\item Comprehensive evaluation demonstrating that Hybrid++ achieves near-DnCNN performance with less than 8\% of its parameters while maintaining full PDE interpretability.
\end{itemize}

\section{Proposed Method: Hybrid++}

\subsection{Multiplicative Gamma Noise Model}
Let $u$ denote the clean grayscale image and $f$ the noisy observation. The multiplicative gamma noise model is:
\begin{equation}
f = u \cdot n, \qquad n \sim \Gamma(L, 1/L),
\end{equation}
where $L$ is the number of looks, yielding $\mathbb{E}[n]=1$ and $\text{Var}(n)=1/L$. Low $L$ (e.g., $L{=}1$) corresponds to severe speckle noise, while higher $L$ (e.g., $L{=}10$) represents milder corruption.

\subsection{Architecture Overview}
Hybrid++ is built directly upon the Trainable Nonlinear Reaction Diffusion (TNRD) framework \cite{chen2017tnrd}, preserving its core mathematical formulation---a sequence of reaction-diffusion stages, each comprising learnable convolution filters, a nonlinear influence function, and a data-fidelity reaction term---while systematically upgrading every component for the multiplicative gamma noise regime. Table~\ref{tab:tnrd_upgrade} summarizes the specific enhancements.

\begin{table}[t]
\caption{TNRD $\to$ Hybrid++: Component-wise Upgrades}
\label{tab:tnrd_upgrade}
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Component & Original TNRD & Hybrid++ \\
\midrule
Initialization & Identity (noisy) & Differentiable PDE \\
Filter input & 1-ch (estimate) & 2-ch (estimate + noisy) \\
Filter bank & 8 single-scale $3{\times}3$ & 64 multiscale ($3{\times}3$+$5{\times}5$) \\
Influence fn. & 1-layer per-ch $\tanh$ & 4-layer with SE attention \\
Noise adapt. & None (separate models) & 64-dim MLP embedding \\
Stages & 7 & 3 \\
Parameters & ${\sim}$21k & ${\sim}$44k \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The architecture consists of two principal components: (1) a \textit{differentiable PDE initialization} that replaces TNRD's identity initialization with a physics-informed initial estimate, and (2) a sequence of $T{=}3$ \textit{enhanced reaction-diffusion stages} that iteratively refine this estimate using the upgraded components described below.

\subsection{Differentiable PDE Initialization}
The process begins with a nonlinear smooth diffusion PDE explicitly designed for multiplicative gamma noise:
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \operatorname{div}\!\left( \left(\frac{u_\sigma}{M}\right)^{\!\alpha} \frac{1}{1+|\nabla u_\sigma|^\beta}\, \nabla u \right),
\label{eq:pde}
\end{equation}
where $u_\sigma$ denotes the Gaussian-smoothed estimate, $M$ is the local maximum intensity for normalization, and $\alpha$, $\beta$ control the intensity-dependent and gradient-dependent diffusion behavior respectively.

\textbf{Key design choice:} Unlike classical PDE approaches where $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are fixed scalars found via exhaustive grid search, Hybrid++ implements these as \texttt{nn.Parameter} tensors. The entire PDE unrolling (20 iterations with step size $\delta_t{=}0.12$) is fully differentiable, allowing the network to jointly optimize the PDE physics alongside the convolutional stages via standard backpropagation. This enables the PDE initialization to automatically adapt its diffusion behavior to the specific noise characteristics encountered during training.

\subsection{Multiscale Filter Banks with Skip Connections}
At each stage $t$, the network receives a 2-channel input formed by concatenating the current estimate $u_{t-1}$ with the original noisy observation $f$. This skip-connection provides continuous access to high-frequency information that might otherwise be lost through successive diffusion steps.

This 2-channel input is processed by two parallel, independently learnable filter banks:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{32 filters of size $3{\times}3{\times}2$}: Capture fine-grained local textures and sharp edge transitions.
\item \textbf{32 filters of size $5{\times}5{\times}2$}: Capture broader spatial context and larger-scale structural patterns.
\end{itemize}

The filter responses are concatenated along the channel dimension, producing a \textbf{64-channel feature map}. This multiscale design is critical for multiplicative gamma noise, where noise patterns overlap heavily with structural high-frequency content---the dual-scale representation allows the network to disambiguate noise from texture at multiple spatial frequencies simultaneously.

All filters are initialized with Kaiming-style scaling ($1/\sqrt{k^2 \cdot c_{in}}$) and are fully learnable throughout training.

\subsection{Dynamic Noise Level Embedding}
To enable blind denoising across varying noise severities, a 3-layer Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) maps the scalar noise strength parameter to a 64-dimensional conditioning vector:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{e} = W_3 \cdot \text{ReLU}(W_2 \cdot \text{ReLU}(W_1 \cdot \sigma_L + b_1) + b_2) + b_3,
\end{equation}
where $\sigma_L = 1/\sqrt{L}$ is the noise strength derived from the gamma distribution's coefficient of variation, and $W_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{32\times1}$, $W_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{64\times32}$, $W_3 \in \mathbb{R}^{64\times64}$.

This 64-dimensional embedding $\mathbf{e}$ is spatially broadcast and added to the 64-channel filter responses at \textit{every} stage, dynamically biasing the feature representation based on noise severity. This design enables a single unified model to handle both $L{=}1$ and $L{=}10$ without separate weights, unlike baseline methods which require training distinct models per noise level.

\subsection{4-Layer SE-Attention Influence Function}
The core of the reaction-diffusion update is the nonlinear influence function $\Phi(\cdot)$, which shapes the diffusion behavior. Hybrid++ introduces a deep, 4-layer architecture with integrated channel attention:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Layer 1:} $1{\times}1$ convolution ($64 \to 64$) followed by $\tanh$ activation.
\item \textbf{Layer 2:} $1{\times}1$ convolution ($64 \to 64$) followed by $\tanh$ activation.
\item \textbf{Layer 3:} Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) block with bottleneck ratio 4:
\begin{equation}
\text{SE}(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{x} \cdot \sigma\!\left(W_{\text{up}} \cdot \text{ReLU}(W_{\text{down}} \cdot \text{GAP}(\mathbf{x}))\right),
\end{equation}
where $W_{\text{down}} \in \mathbb{R}^{16\times64}$ squeezes and $W_{\text{up}} \in \mathbb{R}^{64\times16}$ excites, with $\text{GAP}$ denoting global average pooling and $\sigma$ the sigmoid function. This mechanism dynamically re-weights the 64 channels based on their global importance, acting as a learned attention mechanism.
\item \textbf{Layer 4:} $1{\times}1$ convolution ($64 \to 1$) followed by $\tanh$, collapsing the attended features into a single spatial diffusion map.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Design rationale:} The original TNRD uses a simple 1-layer per-channel influence function. By deepening this to 4 layers with an intermediate SE-attention block, Hybrid++ can learn highly complex, context-dependent nonlinear response mappings. The $\tanh$ activations ensure bounded outputs consistent with the physical diffusion formulation. Critically, \textit{no normalization layers} (e.g., BatchNorm) are used anywhere in the influence function, preserving the mathematical stability of the PDE model.

\subsection{Stage Update Rule}
Each stage combines the influenced diffusion term with a data-fidelity reaction term:
\begin{equation}
u_t = u_{t-1} - w \cdot \Phi\!\left(\mathbf{R}(u_{t-1}, f) + \mathbf{e}\right) - \lambda_t \cdot w \cdot \frac{u_{t-1} - f}{u_{t-1}^2 + \varepsilon},
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{R}(u_{t-1}, f)$ denotes the 64-channel multiscale filter response, $\mathbf{e}$ is the noise embedding, $w$ is the intensity-dependent weight derived from the Gaussian-smoothed estimate, and $\lambda_t$ is a learnable per-stage fidelity parameter. The reaction term derives from the maximum a posteriori (MAP) formulation for multiplicative gamma noise, providing a principled data attachment force.

\subsection{Two-Phase Training Strategy}
\label{sec:training}
Training Hybrid++ employs a two-phase optimization strategy specifically designed for deep unrolled networks:

\textbf{Phase 1: Stage-wise Initialization.} Each of the 3 stages is trained sequentially. When training stage $t$, all prior stages are frozen and only stage $t$'s parameters (along with the PDE and embedding parameters) are optimized. This prevents vanishing gradients through the deeply unrolled computation graph and ensures each stage learns a meaningful refinement step. Training uses Adam with $\text{lr}=10^{-3}$, ReduceLROnPlateau scheduling, early stopping with patience 10, and gradient clipping at max norm 1.0.

\textbf{Phase 2: Joint End-to-End Refinement.} After stage-wise initialization, \textit{all} parameters across all 3 stages, the PDE module, and the noise embedding are unfrozen and jointly optimized for 50 epochs. This phase uses Adam with $\text{lr}=10^{-4}$ and CosineAnnealingLR ($T_{\max}{=}50$, $\eta_{\min}{=}10^{-6}$), allowing the entire network to co-adapt and fine-tune the inter-stage interactions. The loss function throughout both phases is Mean Squared Error (MSE).

\section{Experimental Setup}
\subsection{Dataset}
We use the Fields of Experts (FoE) dataset containing 400 grayscale images of size $180{\times}180$, split into 80\% training, 10\% validation, and 10\% testing (40 test images) with a fixed random seed of 42. During training, random horizontal/vertical flips are applied for data augmentation. For qualitative evaluation on real-world data, we additionally test on 10 real SAR images from the SEN1-2 dataset.

\subsection{Baselines}
We compare Hybrid++ against four representative methods spanning classical, learning-based, and deep learning approaches:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{BM3D} \cite{dabov2007bm3d}: Non-local patch-based method applied via homomorphic (log-domain) transform with noise variance $\sigma^2 = \psi_1(L)$ (trigamma function).
\item \textbf{PDE Baseline}: Classical nonlinear smooth diffusion PDE (Eq.~\ref{eq:pde}) with hand-tuned parameters ($\alpha{=}1.0$, $\beta{=}2.0$).
\item \textbf{TNRD} \cite{chen2017tnrd}: Original 7-stage trainable reaction-diffusion with 8 filters per stage, separate models per noise level.
\item \textbf{DnCNN} \cite{zhang2017beyond}: 17-layer deep CNN with batch normalization (556,097 parameters), representing the unconstrained deep learning upper bound.
\end{itemize}

All learning-based baselines are trained with identical MSE loss, Adam optimizer, and the same dataset split for fair comparison.

\section{Ablation Study}
To justify each architectural design choice in Hybrid++, we conduct a systematic ablation study by progressively removing components from the base TNRD framework.

\begin{table}[t]
\caption{Ablation Study: Impact of Architectural Components}
\label{tab:ablation}
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule
& \multicolumn{2}{c}{$L=1$} & \multicolumn{2}{c}{$L=10$} \\
\cmidrule(lr){2-3} \cmidrule(lr){4-5}
Variant & PSNR & SSIM & PSNR & SSIM \\
\midrule
Full Base TNRD & 18.56 & 0.326 & 23.18 & 0.629 \\
\quad $-$ Skip connections & 17.75 & 0.324 & 23.10 & 0.642 \\
\quad $-$ Nonlinear influence & 10.46 & 0.144 & 17.63 & 0.456 \\
\quad $-$ Reduced filters ($N_k{=}4$) & 16.91 & 0.245 & 21.99 & 0.559 \\
\quad $-$ Reduced stages ($T{=}3$) & 17.06 & 0.289 & 22.24 & 0.578 \\
\midrule
\textbf{Hybrid++ (Full)} & \textbf{20.86} & \textbf{0.463} & \textbf{25.02} & \textbf{0.692} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Table~\ref{tab:ablation} reveals several critical insights:

\textbf{Nonlinear influence functions are essential.} Removing the nonlinear influence triggers a catastrophic ${\sim}8$~dB collapse at $L{=}1$, reducing performance to raw-noise levels. This confirms that learned nonlinear diffusion shaping is the absolute foundation of the model.

\textbf{Skip connections provide crucial grounding.} Removing the noisy-image skip connection drops PSNR by 0.81~dB at $L{=}1$, demonstrating that continuous access to the original observation prevents high-frequency information loss through successive diffusion steps.

\textbf{Filter capacity directly drives performance.} Reducing filters from 8 to 4 drops PSNR by 1.65~dB at $L{=}1$. This directly motivates Hybrid++'s expansion to 64 channels (32$\times$2 multiscale), which enables the dramatic +2.31~dB improvement over the base TNRD.

\textbf{Stage depth has diminishing returns.} Reducing from 7 to 3 stages drops PSNR by 1.50~dB in the base TNRD (with 8 filters). However, Hybrid++ demonstrates that with sufficient filter capacity (64 channels) and deep influence functions, \textit{just 3 stages suffice} to achieve superior performance, confirming that width is more valuable than depth in unrolled PDE networks.

\section{Conclusion}
This paper presented Hybrid++, a physics-informed architecture for multiplicative gamma noise removal that bridges the divide between classical mathematical methods and modern deep learning. By expanding the TNRD framework with differentiable PDE initialization, 64-channel multiscale filter banks, 4-layer SE-attention influence functions, 64-dimensional noise embeddings, and a two-phase training strategy, Hybrid++ achieved 20.86~dB at $L{=}1$ and 25.02~dB at $L{=}10$ on the FoE benchmark. Most significantly, this compact 44,408-parameter model closes the gap to the 556,097-parameter DnCNN to within just 0.23~dB, proving that principled physical priors augmented with targeted deep learning can effectively match the representational capacity of unconstrained black-box networks while maintaining full mathematical interpretability. Qualitative evaluation on real SAR images further confirms the practical applicability of the proposed approach.
